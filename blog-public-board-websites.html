What Makes a Good Public Board Website

A look at the key elements that help communities find meeting information, policies, and updates on your board site.

A public board website serves as the front door for community members who want to understand what their board is doing. When done well, it makes information easy to find and builds trust with the people you serve.

Clear Meeting Information

The most common reason people visit a board website is to find out when meetings happen and what will be discussed. A good board site makes this information prominent and easy to access. This includes upcoming meeting dates, agendas, and any materials that will be reviewed.

After meetings conclude, minutes and any recorded video should be posted promptly. Community members who couldn't attend in person should be able to catch up on what happened.

Organized Policy Library

Policies are often the documents people need most but have the hardest time finding. A well-organized policy section with clear categories and search functionality helps community members locate the information they need without frustration.

Consider how policies are named and grouped. Using plain language and logical categories makes a big difference in whether people can actually find what they're looking for.

Contact and Participation Information

Community members should be able to easily find out how to contact the board, submit public comments, or get involved. This information should be visible and straightforward, not buried in a footer or hidden behind multiple clicks.

Mobile-Friendly Design

Many people will access your board site from their phones. A responsive design that works well on smaller screens ensures that everyone can access the information they need, regardless of what device they're using.

Consistent Updates

A board website is only useful if it's kept current. Outdated meeting information or stale content erodes trust. Establishing a routine for updating the site after each meeting helps ensure the information stays accurate.

Key Takeaways

  • Make meeting information prominent and easy to find
  • Organize policies with clear categories and search
  • Ensure the site works well on mobile devices
  • Keep content updated after each meeting
