COMMON CHALLENGES

Sound Familiar?

These are the governance headaches we hear about every day from boards just like yours.

"I can't find that email..."

Critical documents buried in email threads. Board members asking for the same attachment three times. No single source of truth.

"Our PDFs aren't accessible"

Accessibility lawsuits are real. Screen readers can't parse your policy documents. Mobile users struggle to read anything.

"Meeting prep takes forever"

Late nights assembling board packets. Chasing down signatures. Manually updating multiple calendars and websites.

"New members are lost"

Onboarding takes months. Historical decisions are scattered. Institutional knowledge walks out the door with departing members.

"The public can't find anything"

Your website is outdated. Meeting info is hard to locate. Residents call the office for basic information that should be online.

"Board members won't speak up"

Difficult topics get avoided. Honest feedback is rare. You never know what members really think until it's too late.

REAL-WORLD USE CASES

How BoardBlocs Helps
With Board Governance

From day-to-day operations to long-term governance strategy, here's how boards use BoardBlocs to work more effectively.

Preparing for Monthly Board Meetings

Instead of emailing PDFs back and forth, administrators build the agenda directly in BoardBlocs. Documents from Google Drive or Microsoft 365 are linked—not re-uploaded—so everyone always sees the latest version. Board members get notified when packets are ready, and they can review materials on their phone during their commute.

Agenda Building Document Sharing Mobile Access

Publishing Accessible Policy Documents

A school district needs to publish their updated student handbook. Instead of posting a 50-page PDF that fails accessibility checks, they use DocMersion to transform it into a searchable, mobile-friendly web page. Parents can read it on any device, screen readers work perfectly, and the district avoids ADA compliance issues.

DocMersion ADA Compliance Public Access

Managing Committee Work Between Meetings

A finance committee needs to review budget proposals before the next full board meeting. They have their own private workspace with dedicated document storage, a discussion thread, and their own calendar. Members can collaborate asynchronously without flooding the main board's communication channels.

Committees Private Workspaces Collaboration

Creating a Public Board Website

A city council wants residents to easily find meeting schedules, watch live streams, and access approved minutes. BoardBlocs creates a branded public site at board.cityname.gov that matches their main website. The public calendar shows upcoming meetings, past recordings are archived, and residents can submit public comments online.

Public Sites Transparency Live Streams

Onboarding New Board Members

When a new member joins the board, they don't have to dig through years of emails. Everything is in one place: past meeting minutes, current policies, committee structures, and ongoing discussions. They can search across all documents, catch up on recent decisions, and get up to speed in days instead of months.

Onboarding Search History

Getting Honest Feedback on Difficult Topics

A nonprofit board needs to discuss a sensitive personnel matter. Using avatar-based discussions, members can share their honest opinions without the social pressure of being identified. The system tracks sentiment and enthusiasm levels, giving leadership insight into how the board really feels—not just what they're willing to say out loud.

Avatar Discussions Sentiment Analysis Honest Feedback
